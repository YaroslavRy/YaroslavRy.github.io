Delta

The delta rhythm has the lowest frequency (1–4Hz) and highest amplitude of the classical brainwaves. Delta waves begin to appear in stage 3: sleep state, and by stage 4 nearly all EEG activity is dominated by delta waves. In the awake state, delta waves may be involved in integration of cerebral activity with homeostatic processes, and are prominent in several disorders such as tinnitus (Chapter 10). Considerable evidence also suggests that delta oscillations are implicated in attention, salience detection, motivation, and subliminal perception (Knyazev, 2012; Chapter 4). Delta oscillations also appear to drive temporal prediction accuracy (Arnal et al., 2015) and successful speech understanding (Steinmetzger and Rosen, 2017; Chapter 3).
